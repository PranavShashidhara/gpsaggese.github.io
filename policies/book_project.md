Dear past and present TAs, RAs, best class students,

I wanted to thank you for helping me build the two classes,
"[DATA605: Big Data Systems](https://github.com/gpsaggese-org/umd_classes/tree/master/data605/lectures)"
and
"[MSML610: Advanced Machine Learning](https://github.com/gpsaggese-org/umd_classes/tree/master/msml610/lectures)"
in the last 4 years.

1. This year, the two classes combined have almost 400 students across many
   sessions and different teachers! The enrollment has grown 5x in 4 years, and
   the classes have a 4.7 / 5 rating in the official UMD surveys. Students like
   the classes so much that UMD has decided to offer them online as well.
2. I've been contacted by a few publishers to turn the classes into textbooks. I
   think it's a good idea, since the major complaint from students (besides my
   accent and the jokes against our enlightened tech billionaires) is that the
   classes refer to more than 20 books, and students go crazy to find support
   besides the slides
3. Since each of you has helped in making the class a success, it's fair to
   share the success with you all.
4. The success will not be monetary, since I asked the publishers to make the
   books available for free or at the cost of production (printing, marketing,
   etc). The publishers thought I was insane and gladly agreed with my request.
5. Here is the deal: everybody who contributes to turning the classes into books
   will have their name as the author of the chapters they contribute to.
6. The books will be in a format mixing a book and a Jupyter notebook
   full-of-widgets, where there is the minimal amount of theory and definitions,
   and plenty of examples of those concepts implemented in Python, that you can
   interact with and get your hands dirty. The current prototype looks like a
   [Jupyter book](https://jupyterbook.org/en/stable/intro.html)
7. Good news: ~70% of the work is already done.
8. Bad news: work still needs to be done. What I need help with is:
   1. Add more tutorials for the missing topics (TBD)
   2. Suggest improvements and ideas for new chapters
   3. Add content
   4. Improve the text
   5. Improve the figures
   6. Read and summarize state-of-the-art articles
   7. ...

Rules of engagement:

- Only work on Saturday and Sunday or outside work hours (you have day jobs or
  you are in school, and those need to be the priorities)
- All the work happens on a public GitHub through PRs with the usual approach we
  know and love
- We will have a weekly sync (outside of work hours)
- No material compensation besides immortal glory
- Once you commit (even a few hours a week to the project), you need to do your
  best to honor the commitment — of course, emergency and class exams are ok,
  but the team needs to know they can count on you
- Be uncomfortable with something new and learn
- Enjoy the process

Most of you have already starting helping, but to clarify, if you are
interested, reply to this email with the class or classes you want to help with
and with how many hours per week you plan to invest in this project (this is to
get a sense of the tasks).
